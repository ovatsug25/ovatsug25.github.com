The State of Data in the Dominican Republic

Through the ONE (Oficina Nacional de Estadistica), the Dominican Republic offers a variety of databases, 43 to be exact.

Many of could have been merged into one. For instance, there are seperate databases Muertes Accidentales y Violetas (Accidental and Violent Deaths) for Drowning, Electrecution, homicide, traffic accidents and suicides. There are then versions for both 2008 and 2009. If we merge these together by year, we come down to 35 different datasets, though I would much prefer to bring it down to 34 and include these in just 1.

Same goes for permitted works in the Private Sector, Estadistacs Vitales (Births, Deaths, Divorce), Company directories, and the Household Surveys for 2005, 2006, 2007.

We must not that this is a new iniative. Having started in December of 2013, it is nice to see that this is still being updated, the last update having occured 07 Aug, 2014 (Interestingly uploaded with Aug as in August and not Ago as in Agosto as the mainly Spanish site would have you believe. (There was also an update on the 1st of July)
