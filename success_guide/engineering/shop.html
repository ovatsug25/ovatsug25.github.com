The author of this guide is a very inexperienced shop worker. I do not know the jargon of the machine shop. I also do no really know how to build anything with it.

Despite that, I compiled a list of link, presented in the order in which they will probably be most useful. MIT has an amazing set of walkthrough through a shop and I suggest you pay attention. A mistake in the shop could literally cost you your finger.

If you pay attention, I’m sure you could go through this pretty successfully though.

MIT TechTV Machine Shop Videos -This seems to be a really comprehensive, well-put together compilation of how to work with and recognize the tools in a machine shop. This is the only webpage I found that organizes the links in such a helpful fashion.

Machine Shop Tools -Very simple. It just provides the name and picture of one of the tools. Good place to spend a few minutes before walking in. Might even be good to make a quick set of flashcards from this. The Machine Shop is very scary for many of those starting out because it can be very dangerous!

Metal Cutting Physics -This is just a specific link to another MIT website that shows a diagram of what happens when you cut. If you can start thinking about things this way, it will be a little easier to get what you want.

Hoss Machine shop videos -This is someone else’s version of this page. Provides links to stuff that does calculations for you. Also gives you a good template for keeping track of parts costs.

Franklin W. Olin College machine shop resources -Definitely not a place to start, though it does contain some pretty nifty blueprints for things you might want to build on your own someday.
