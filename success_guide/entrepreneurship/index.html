Entrepreneurship is a very tedious word to spell. (Notice the French e’s and u’s which pop up in all the wrong places.)

It is however, quite an exciting concept. The literal definition of it is an undertaking, however in many ways, and entrepreneuer is like a demi-god. They can create something from nothing and have no reference point for what their product is to become.

They are creating a new future and usually end up changing the world in small, medium or huge size ways.

Oftentimes, entrepreneures become business-owners. For instance, Google became a business quite by chance. Sergey and Larry originally built the search engine to make their own perosnal lives much easier.

The topics under this section are arranged like so because of entreprenurships frequent association with business. But really all components here are part of entreprenurship. In fact, becoming technical is a thing most entreprenuers have to do just to build their own product. Mark Zuckerberg, the founder of Facebook, was not a computer science major at Harvard. Quite to the contrary, he started out in the classics department and he hoped to be a master in Latin.

So entreprenurship is more like knowing what your needs are and finding a way to satiate them.

So the topics like accoutning, cold-calling and marketing are really just there so that you can have even more skills to manage this.

These are extentions of self.
